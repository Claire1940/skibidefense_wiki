<Section icon={Zap} title="Cloning Mechanics and Red Energy Management">

At the heart of the new **skibi defense units** is the cloning system. Units like the standard Pencil and the Chief Pencil Guy do not just attack; they replicate other units on the field. This process is fueled by "Red," a resource that regenerates over time. Managing your Max Red capacity and Regen rate is the difference between a temporary distraction and a permanent wall of fire.

The cloning duration increases significantly with unit upgrades. For instance, a base Pencil unit starts with a short clone duration, but as you invest in upgrades, the clones persist longer on the battlefield, allowing for overlapping damage windows. This is particularly effective when cloning high-value targets like Upgraded Colossal Clock or Gman, though the Red cost for such elite units is substantially higher.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Summoning Units and Swarm Tactics">

The Pencil Helicopter and Large Pencil Summoner represent a different side of the **skibi defense units** roster. Instead of cloning existing towers, these units summon independent "Pencil Men" to the front lines. These summons act as mobile blockers and damage dealers, moving toward the enemy spawn point to engage targets before they reach your main defensive line.

The Pencil Helicopter is particularly unique due to its line attack, a new mechanic for the game. It summons various members of the "Pencil Crew," providing a constant stream of fodder to distract and damage incoming waves. While their individual DPS might seem low compared to C Pen, the collective damage of a summoned swarm can effectively stall even the fastest enemies.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimal Placement and Synergies">

To get the most out of your **skibi defense units**, placement is everything. Because many Pencil units utilize line attacks or AOE hammers, they should be placed at corners or at the start of long straight paths. The Large Hammer Pencil, for example, has an AOE width of 10 meters, making it incredibly effective when enemies are bunched up at a turn.

Synergy is also vital. Pairing a Chief Pencil Guy with a high-damage unit like a Titan allows you to effectively double your strongest unit's presence for a fraction of the original cost. However, you must ensure your Red energy regeneration can keep up with the expiration of the clones.
