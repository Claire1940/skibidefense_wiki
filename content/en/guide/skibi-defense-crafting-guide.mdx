<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding the Skibi Defense Crafting Guide Mechanics">

The crafting and evolution system in Skibi Defense is the primary gateway to transitioning from early-game survival to late-game dominance. Unlike standard tower defense games where you simply purchase upgrades mid-match, this experience requires players to collect base units, level them up through active gameplay, and fuse them in the main lobby. This mechanic ensures that even common units retain long-term value, serving as essential ingredients for high-tier fusions.

To initiate a craft, players must visit the Evolution Portal located in the main lobby. The system matches specific base units with a set amount of in-game currency (Coins and Gems) to output a significantly stronger variant. Fused units do not just receive a baseline stat upgrade; they unlock entirely new attack patterns, larger placement limits, and specialized passive abilities that are vital for tackling higher difficulty waves.

| Base Unit | Required Level | Evolution Result | Key Stat Boost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cameraman | Level 10 | Large Cameraman | Single-target DPS |
| Large Cameraman | Level 25 | Titan Cameraman | Heavy AoE Cleave |
| Large TV Man | Level 25 | Titan TV Man | Crowd Control Stun |

Successfully utilizing this system requires careful planning. If you rush into fusing your units too early without securing temporary replacements, you may find your active loadout severely weakened. Always maintain a secondary roster of mid-tier defenders to protect your base while you gather resources for your next major upgrade.

<Callout type="warning" title="Level Requirements">
Always level up your base units to the maximum threshold before attempting fusion to ensure optimal stat carryover.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Farming Routes & Material Optimization">

To keep up with the steep currency demands of late-game crafting, players must optimize their farming routes. Different maps offer varying levels of coin and gem yields based on their difficulty and layout complexity. Choosing the right map for your current power level prevents unnecessary defeats and maximizes your hourly resource income.

Choke points and path length play a massive role in how easily you can defend a map while running an economy-focused strategy. Maps with long, winding paths give your towers more time to deal damage, allowing you to place fewer defensive units and invest more early-wave cash into your economy generators.

| Map Name | Difficulty | Coin Yield | Enemy Pattern | Optimal Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Beginner Map | Easy | 500 per run | Low HP fast waves | Economy-focused early defense |
| Mid-Game Map | Normal | 1200 per run | Mixed ground and ranged | Balanced tower placement with AoE |
| Advanced Map | Hard | 2500 per run | High HP armored units | High DPS single-target with stuns |
| Endgame Map | Insane | 5000 per run | Fast boss waves | Full optimized synergy loadouts |

When farming, always prioritize speed over absolute wave completion if your goal is raw coins per hour. Surviving to wave 40 on an Advanced Map quickly often yields more resources per minute than struggling to reach wave 50 on an Insane Map. Adjust your farming targets based on your current roster's capabilities.
